The Origin of Chapati

Chapati, also known as roti, is a traditional unleavened flatbread that originated in the Indian subcontinent. It has been a staple food for centuries, enjoyed across India, Pakistan, and East Africa. The word "chapati" comes from the Hindi word "chapat," meaning "to slap," referring to the way the dough is flattened by hand.

Introduced to East Africa by Indian traders and settlers, chapati has become an essential part of Swahili cuisine. It is often served with stews, vegetables, or beans and has a special place in festive and daily meals.

Ingredients

To make soft chapati, you need:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (or whole wheat flour)
  • 3/4 cup water (adjust as needed)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon oil or melted butter
  • Extra flour for rolling

How to Make Soft Chapati

Instructions:

  1. Mix the Dry Ingredients: Combine 3 cups flour and 1 tsp salt in a bowl.
  2. Add Water and Knead: Gradually add warm water while kneading for 10 minutes.
  3. Rest the Dough: Cover and let it rest for 30 minutes to improve texture.
  4. Divide and Roll: Cut the dough into balls and roll them into thin circles.
  5. Cook on a Pan: Heat a pan, cook each chapati for 30-40 seconds per side, brushing with oil.
  6. Serve Hot: Enjoy with your favorite stew or curry!
